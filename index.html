Henry MacDiarmid

Élan Vital
More Light No More
June 2021
To see the exhibition in person and to purchase artworks, email us.

Locked entrance in the pantry to steep stairs. Dirt floors, exposed pipes, wires, bricks, deteriorating. A hallway and two rooms. Ceiling furnace. A shelf holding an open microwave with old bud light cans. A cup more dust than cup. A painted rag stretched to a square. Plaster casts of cardboard boxes. A ghost with multiple reflections. A makeshift skylight in my head. "...we shall see the imagination build "walls" of impalpable shadows, comfort itself with the illusion of protection–or, just the contrary, tremble behind thick walls, mistrust the staunchest ramparts." Shadows and whispers go hand in hand. A found photograph of a horse basking. A dead bird laying on the ground, branches scattered. Everything has been teleported here. Broken flower pots fossiled in dirt. 37047734 upside down. Fake constellations everywhere. A room with a door that has locks on the outside. A drawer filled with secrets they would like to tell. Elements in the body. Staring out windows that aren't there. Dark daydreams.

Henry MacDiarmid was born and raised in Reno, Nevada where he currently lives and works. He graduated with a Bachelor’s of Fine Arts degree in Studio Art with an emphasis in Photography in 2018 from the University of Nevada, Reno. He has exhibited in solo and group exhibitions at the Holland Project in Reno, Nevada from 2015 to 2018, and in a group exhibition at Plexus Projects in Brooklyn, New York in 2018. He organizes and curates with Fiona McElhany for the Reno, Nevada gallery, window mine.
